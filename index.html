What is neuromorphic computing?



Neuromorphic computing, A.K.A neuromorphic engineering, is a form of computing that mimics the way the human brain works. Neuromorphic computing uses hardware and software that replicate neural and synaptic structures/functions of the brain to process information. Currently, neuromorphic computing is still being researched.

How does neuromorphic technology work?

The process and function of neuromorphic computing borrows heavily from the rules of biology and neuroscience. To understand how neuromorphic computing works, it is important to understand neurons and how they work. Neurons are the fundamental units of the nervous system and the brain. Nerve cells act as messengers and send information throughout the brain and to parts of the body. To send information, a neuron must “spike” to become active which triggers the release of chemical and electrical signals. These signals travel through connected points in the network called synapses. This allows neurons to communicate with each other.


This specific mechanism is replicated in neuromorphic computing using SNNs, spiking neural networks. A spiking neural network is a type of artificial neural network which includes spiking neurons and synapses. Unlike regular artificial neurons that continuously send numerical values, spiking neurons only send signals (called “spikes”) when their internal value (or voltage) reaches a certain limit, similar to how neurons in the brain send signals only when they are activated enough.


Basically, each neuron has a value which changes over time based on incoming signals from other neurons. The neuron will “spike” and send a signal to connected neurons once it receives enough neurons to reach the limit. After it does, the value of the neuron will reset and the process repeats.


Synapses connect neurons and create pathways. They also have values, which are different from the way neuron values function. Synapse values determine the strength of a signal. These values can adapt and change over time through learning, meaning the system adapts and improves by strengthening or weakening connections based on experience.

Real-world applications


Autonomous vehicles

Self-driving vehicles


Neuromorphic computing can be used to improve navigational skills in self-driving vehicles, due to its strengths in adapting and pattern recognition.


Cybersecurity



Neuromorphic computing has strengths in adaptability and pattern recognition which makes it useful for cybersecurity, as it can detect unusual patterns that could indicate breaches or potential cyberattacks. Since the neuromorphic system is so swift, these attacks can easily be defended against with neuromorphic devices.


Robotics & AI



Neuromorphic computing can be used to improve specific robotic skills, such as real-time learning, decision making, recognition skills, navigation skills, etc. Not only will it help improve robotics skills, but it will also enhance AI’s ability to recognize patterns, use less energy, use more logic, etc.

Social impacts

The development of neuromorphic computing is seen as a revolutionary approach to computing as it enables machines to process information and learn in ways the human brain can. It holds the promise of transforming industries, enhancing the capabilities of AI, and more. The development of neuromorphic computing will shift the way people approach computing, as it offers an entirely new way of computing.


Neuromorphic computing is predicted to be used in robotics, the enhancement of AI, and even healthcare, for which it’s being studied for its predictive analysis. It may be able to analyse large quantities of medical data, and identify patterns which can lead to diagnosing disease earlier. In healthcare, neuromorphic computing will accelerate the decision making process and improve patient help by allowing quicker treatment.



Currently, AI, especially large language models, use tons of electricity to function. The use of neuromorphic computing in AI will help reduce the amount of electricity AI uses, as neuromorphic computing is incredibly energy efficient. Neuromorphic computing will also help improve AI skills, such as learning skills, recognition skills, and more.
